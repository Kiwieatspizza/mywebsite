What is depression?

Depression is a common mental disorder. Globally, it is estimated that 5% of adults suffer from the disorder. It is characterized by persistent sadness and a lack of interest or pleasure in previously rewarding or enjoyable activities. It can also disturb sleep and appetite. Tiredness and poor concentration are common.

Symptoms

Types of major depression include melancholia, psychotic and antenatal or postnatal. You may be diagnosed with mild, moderate or severe depression. How to help

Education, lifestyle changes, social support and psychological therapy are important treatments for depression. People may also require antidepressant medication. Medications may take up to six weeks to be effective, so be patient.
